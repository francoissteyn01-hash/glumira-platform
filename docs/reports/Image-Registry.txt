## 1. PURPOSE

This document governs how all Mira education content
is researched, verified, written, and maintained.

No content enters the Mira education system
without passing through this framework.

Medical accuracy is not negotiable.
A child or caregiver who acts on incorrect information
from GluMira™ could be harmed.
That is the standard we hold ourselves to.

## 3. CONTENT ACCURACY CHECKLIST

Every piece of Mira education content must pass
all items on this checklist before publication.

### 3.1 Medical Accuracy
[ ] Claim verified against at least one Gold Standard source
[ ] Claim verified against at least one Secondary source
[ ] No outdated guideline used — check publication date
[ ] Insulin names verified as currently available
[ ] Glucose targets verified as current ISPAD recommendation
[ ] No extrapolation beyond what the evidence supports
[ ] No simplification that creates a clinical misconception

### 3.2 Age Appropriateness
[ ] Language verified appropriate for target age band
[ ] Sentence length appropriate — Band 2 max 12 words per sentence
[ ] No abstract concepts introduced before Band 3
[ ] No fear-inducing language at any age band
[ ] No complication content in 0 to 12 framework

### 3.3 Emotional Safety
[ ] Content reviewed for anxiety-inducing language
[ ] No language implying failure or blame
[ ] Hopeful closing statement included
[ ] Caregiver parallel track does not bleed into child track
[ ] Content tested against — would this make a scared child
    more scared or less scared

### 3.4 Legal and Regulatory
[ ] Medical disclaimer present where required
[ ] No dosing recommendations made
[ ] No device-specific instructions that could be misapplied
[ ] Content does not replace clinical advice — stated clearly
[ ] Version number and review date recorded

## 4. CONTENT DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

### Step 1 — Topic Brief
Define the topic, target age band, learning objective,
and emotional goal before writing begins.

Template:
```
Topic:
Age Band:
Learning Objective — what the child will understand after:
Emotional Goal — how the child will feel after:
Primary Source:
Secondary Source:
Mira Voice Notes — any specific tone considerations:
```

### Step 2 — First Draft
Written in Mira's voice as defined in
02_GluMira-Taglines-Statements-V7.md

Rules for first draft:
- Write for the child, not for the parent
- Write for the ear, not the eye — read it aloud
- One concept only — never two in one lesson
- Start with acknowledgement, end with capability

### Step 3 — Medical Review
Compare every factual claim against primary source.
Mark each claim with its source reference.
Any claim that cannot be sourced is removed or rewritten.

### Step 4 — Language Review
Read the content aloud as if speaking to a child
in the target age band.
Ask:
- Would a child in this age band understand every word
- Does any sentence feel scary
- Does Mira sound like herself — warm, honest, present
- Is the closing statement genuinely hopeful

### Step 5 — Caregiver Track Development
Write the parallel caregiver version.
This can be more clinical and detailed.
Must reference the same primary sources.
Must include practical action items for the caregiver.

### Step 6 — Final Review
Full read-through against the accuracy checklist.
Sign off with version number and date.
File in GluMira-V7/07_BRAND/Education/

## 5. DIFFICULT TOPICS — HANDLING GUIDE

These topics require special care.
Each has specific rules for how Mira addresses them.

### 5.1 The Diagnosis Moment
The hardest moment. Child may be in hospital.
Family is in shock.
Mira does not rush to explain.
Mira sits with the feeling first.
Rule: First two sentences are always about feelings.
Never about facts.

### 5.2 Injections and Pain
Children are afraid of needles.
Mira acknowledges this directly.
She does not say it does not hurt.
She says it gets easier and explains why.
Rule: Never minimise. Always validate. Always progress.

### 5.3 Being Different From Friends
This is a deep fear for ages 5 to 12.
Mira addresses it directly and honestly.
She does not say it does not matter.
She says it matters AND it does not define them.
Rule: Acknowledge the feeling. Offer perspective.
Never dismiss.

### 5.4 Hypos at School or in Public
Embarrassment is real and valid.
Mira validates embarrassment without amplifying it.
She gives the child language to use with friends.
Rule: Provide scripts. Give the child words.
Preparation reduces embarrassment.

### 5.5 Caregiver Anxiety Visible to the Child
Children see their parents' fear.
Mira acknowledges that the people who love them
are also learning.
Rule: Normalise caregiver emotion without amplifying
child anxiety. Frame caregiver vigilance as love.

### 5.6 Device Failure or Sensor Error
Child may panic when device shows ERROR or ???
Mira teaches calm response protocol.
Rule: Error is not emergency. Check. Wait. Tell a grown-up.
Never panic first.

### 5.7 Sick Days
Illness raises glucose and complicates management.
Mira explains sick day rules at Band 3 level only.
Younger children are directed to tell a grown-up immediately.
Rule: Band 1 and 2 — tell a grown-up and rest.
Band 3 — here is what happens and here is what to do.

## 6. LANGUAGE GUIDE — APPROVED AND PROHIBITED

### 6.1 Approved Language
| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| Diabetic | Person with diabetes / child with Type 1 |
| Control | Manage |
| Normal blood sugar | Target range |
| Good number / bad number | In range / out of range |
| Failed to control | Glucose went higher than target |
| You should have | Next time we can try |
| This is serious | This needs attention |
| You will be fine | Most children manage this really well |

### 6.2 Prohibited Words in Child Content
- Cure
- Permanent
- Damage
- Blind
- Amputation
- Kidney failure
- Death
- Dangerous (use needs attention instead)
- Your fault
- You did wrong
- Bad control
- Poor management
- Chronic (use ongoing or lifelong instead)

### 6.3 Numbers in Child Content
- Always use mmol/L OR mg/dL depending on region setting
- Never use both in the same sentence
- Target ranges stated as a range — never a single number
- Insulin doses never stated — always referred to a clinician

| University paediatric endocrinology units | Academic review | Phase 2 |

### 7.2 Lived Experience Panel
All Mira content is reviewed by at least one person
with lived experience of Type 1 diabetes before publication.
This means a parent, caregiver, or adult with T1D
who can verify the emotional accuracy of the content.
Academic accuracy without emotional accuracy is not enough.
