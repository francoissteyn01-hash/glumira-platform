## 1. MARKETING PHILOSOPHY

GluMira™ does not sell.
GluMira™ educates, empowers, and earns trust.

Every piece of marketing must do one of three things:
1. Teach something useful about Type 1 diabetes management
2. Show a real problem GluMira™ solves
3. Make a caregiver, child, or clinician feel understood

We never shout. We never pressure.
We show up consistently and we earn the right to be trusted.

---

## 2. PRIMARY TARGET AUDIENCES

### 2.1 Tier 1 — Most Important
These are the people who make the decision to use GluMira™.

**Caregivers of children with Type 1 Diabetes**
- Parents, grandparents, guardians
- Predominantly mothers in the primary caregiver role
- Age range: 25–55
- Exhausted, anxious, under-informed by the system
- They search at 2am for answers
- They need to feel understood before they trust a product
- Key pain point: "I don't know why the numbers do what they do"
- Key emotion: Fear mixed with fierce love
- Where to reach them: Facebook groups, Instagram,
  WhatsApp communities, pediatric diabetes forums

**Children aged 0–12 newly diagnosed**
- They do not choose GluMira™ — their caregivers do
- But Mira must speak directly to them
- They need to not be afraid
- They need to feel seen and not different
- Key emotion: Confusion, fear, wanting to be normal
- Where to reach them: Through their caregivers first,
  then through Mira inside the platform

### 2.2 Tier 2 — High Value
These audiences drive clinical adoption and credibility.

**Pediatric Endocrinologists**
- They recommend platforms to families
- One clinician = many patients
- Key pain point: No tool gives them clean pattern visibility
  without asking families to bring printouts
- Key message: "Your patients are already logging.
  GluMira™ makes that data readable for you."
- Where to reach them: ISPAD, medical conferences,
  LinkedIn, direct outreach

**Pediatric Diabetes Nurses and Educators**
- Front line of newly diagnosed education
- They are the ones who hand families information
  on the day of diagnosis
- Key message: "Give them Mira on the hardest day."
- Where to reach them: Hospital diabetes education units,
  LinkedIn, nursing associations

**General Practitioners and Family Doctors**
- Not specialists but often first contact
- Need simple pattern summaries not data dumps
- Key message: "One page. Everything you need to know."

### 2.3 Tier 3 — Growth Audiences
**Schools and Educational Institutions**
- School nurses, teachers, administrators
- Need a care plan that works and is legally defensible
- Key message: "The school care plan that writes itself."

**Researchers**
- Academic institutions studying Type 1 diabetes
- Need clean anonymised cohort data
- Key message: "Real world data. Consistent methodology."

**Diabetes Organisations and NGOs**
- JDRF, Diabetes South Africa, IDF
- Partnership and endorsement targets
- Key message: "Built for the patients your mission serves."

## 3. THE DEXCOM G7 ANGLE

### 3.1 Why This Matters Now
Dexcom G7 has been approved for children as young as 2 years.
This is a market-expanding event for GluMira™.

More children on CGM = more families needing
a platform that makes CGM data meaningful.
Raw Dexcom data without context is noise.
GluMira™ powered by IOB Hunter™ turns that noise into insight.

### 3.2 The Message
> "Dexcom G7 shows you the number.
> GluMira™ shows you why."

> "Your child is now on Dexcom G7.
> Now get the platform that tells you what to do with the data."

### 3.3 The Positioning
- GluMira™ is the intelligence layer on top of Dexcom G7
- Not a competitor — a companion
- Position as the essential second step after CGM adoption
- Target every family that just started on Dexcom G7

### 3.4 Clinical Channel
- Approach pediatric endocrinologists who prescribe Dexcom G7
- Offer a free clinical license during beta
- Ask them to recommend GluMira™ alongside Dexcom G7
  at the point of prescription

## 5. MARKETING CHANNELS

### 5.1 Owned Channels — Build First
These cost nothing but time and must be built before
paid marketing begins.

**Platform itself**
- Every logged-in user who shares data becomes an advocate
- Streak system drives daily return and word of mouth
- Badge system creates shareable moments

**Email**
- Onboarding sequence — 7 emails over 14 days
- Weekly insight digest for Pro and AI users
- Monthly education newsletter — free to all users
- No spam. No daily emails. Respect the inbox.

**Website — glumira.ai**
- Education blog — SEO driven

## 6. CONTENT PILLARS

Every piece of content falls under one of these 5 pillars.
This keeps the brand consistent across all channels.

### Pillar 1 — Education
Teach something useful. No sales.
Examples:
- What is insulin on board and why does it matter
- How the dawn phenomenon affects children differently
- What to tell the school nurse on day one

### Pillar 2 — Empathy
Make the caregiver feel understood.
Examples:
- The 2am glucose check — you are not alone
- When the numbers make no sense and you are exhausted
- What a bad diabetes week looks like and why it happens

### Pillar 3 — Feature Visibility
Show what GluMira™ does — without selling.
Examples:
- Here is what IOB Hunter™ shows you after a meal
- Here is how the pattern intelligence flagged a trend
- Here is what a school care plan looks like

### Pillar 4 — Mira & Children
Content that speaks to and about children with Type 1.
Examples:
- Mira explains what insulin does — for ages 5 to 8
- Badges children have earned this week
- Stories from the waiting room game

### Pillar 5 — Community & Trust
Build credibility through association and advocacy.
Examples:
- Partnership announcements
- Beta user milestones — anonymised
- Grant applications submitted
- ISPAD and JDRF alignment content

## 10. WHAT WE DO NOT DO

- No paid advertising before 500 organic users
- No influencer marketing without clinical credibility
- No partnerships with companies that conflict with
  our medical accuracy standards
- No marketing that uses fear as a primary motivator
- No marketing in channels where children under 13
  are the primary audience — Mira reaches children
  through their caregivers only
