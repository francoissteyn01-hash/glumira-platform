## 1. PHILOSOPHY

Social media is not a billboard.
It is a conversation.

GluMira™ social media exists to:
- Build trust before the app is needed
- Educate caregivers before they know they need us
- Make children with Type 1 feel seen and not alone
- Give clinicians a reason to recommend us
- Build a community that exists whether or not
  someone is a paying user

We post with purpose.
We never post just to post.
Quality over frequency — always.

## 3. CONTENT PILLARS — SOCIAL VERSION

All posts fall under one of these 5 pillars.
Every pillar has a visual style and tone locked below.

### Pillar 1 — EDUCATION
**Purpose:** Teach one useful thing per post
**Tone:** Clear, precise, warm — never condescending
**Visual style:** Navy background, white text,
amber data highlight, clean minimal layout
**Frequency:** 3 posts per week across platforms
**Examples:**
- What is insulin on board — explained in 60 seconds
- Why the dawn phenomenon hits hardest between 3am and 6am
- The difference between ISF and ICR — and why both matter
- What Dexcom G7 data looks like without context
  vs with IOB Hunter™

### Pillar 2 — EMPATHY
**Purpose:** Make caregivers feel understood
**Tone:** Honest, warm, never sentimental or performative
**Visual style:** Soft navy fade, single powerful line of text,
no data — just words
**Frequency:** 2 posts per week
**Examples:**
- "You checked at 2am. You corrected at 3am.
   You were at work by 7am. That is extraordinary."
- "A bad diabetes week is not a failure.
   It is Tuesday."
- "The number on the screen is not a grade.
   It is information."
- "You didn't choose this life.
   You just chose to show up for it anyway."

### Pillar 3 — FEATURE VISIBILITY
**Purpose:** Show what GluMira™ does — without selling
**Tone:** Confident, factual, zero hype
**Visual style:** App UI mockup, navy frame,
feature highlighted with amber accent
**Frequency:** 2 posts per week
**Examples:**
- IOB Hunter™ showing active insulin after a meal
- Pattern intelligence flagging a recurring 3am dip
- School care plan auto-generating in one click
- Downgrade screen — showing data is never deleted

### Pillar 4 — MIRA & CHILDREN
**Purpose:** Content for and about children with Type 1
**Tone:** Gentle, honest, brave, never patronising
**Visual style:** Mira illustration, warm navy,
amber badge glow, child-friendly typography
**Frequency:** 2 posts per week
**Examples:**
- Mira explains what insulin does — age 5 to 8 version
- Badge of the week — anonymous child achievement
- Mira waiting room game preview
- "What Mira says when it's hard" — quote card

### Pillar 5 — COMMUNITY & TRUST
**Purpose:** Build credibility, mark milestones, acknowledge partners
**Tone:** Professional, grateful, grounded — never boastful
**Visual style:** Clean navy, GluMira™ logo prominent,
milestone number in amber
**Frequency:** 1 post per week or milestone-driven
**Examples:**
- Beta milestone — 50 families onboarded
- Partnership announcement — ISPAD alignment
- Grant application submitted
- Dexcom G7 integration live
- Build block completed — glass filling visual

### 4.2 Rules
- Never post the same content on all platforms
  on the same day without adapting it
- LinkedIn posts are longer — 150 to 300 words
- Instagram captions are shorter — 50 to 100 words
- TikTok scripts are 30 to 60 seconds spoken
- Facebook posts include a question to drive comments
- X posts are single punchy lines — max 2 sentences

## 8. ENGAGEMENT RULES

### 8.1 We Always
- Reply to every comment within 24 hours
- Acknowledge every share of our content
- Follow back accounts in the Type 1 diabetes community
- Thank clinicians who engage publicly
- Repost caregiver stories — with permission only

### 8.2 We Never
- Argue with negative comments — we respond once,
  with facts, calmly, then we stop
- Delete negative comments unless they are abusive
- Buy followers or engagement
- Post about competitors
- Make political statements
- Respond to trolls

### 8.3 Crisis Response
If a post receives negative medical feedback:
1. Do not delete the post
2. Add a clarifying comment immediately
3. Pin the clarification
4. Review the post with the brand standards document
5. If factually wrong — correct publicly and apologise

## 9. SOCIAL MEDIA AND THE BUILD

The glass indicator (#TheGlassIsFilling series) connects
the social media build to the platform build.

Every time a build block is completed:
- Post the glass indicator showing the new level
- Name the block that was just completed
- Invite the community to watch the next drop fall

This makes the community part of the build.
They are not waiting for a launch.
They are watching it happen.
